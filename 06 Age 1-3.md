# Age 1–3: Agency, Attention, and the Language Explosion

← [[05 Age 0-1]] | [[00 Education Framework|Index]] | Next: [[07 Age 4-7]]

**Current relevance:** 2-year-old is here now.

---

## Developmental Reality

Language is exploding. The first assertion of autonomous will ("no") is developmental, not misbehavior. Symbolic thinking is emerging — objects can represent other objects.

**Primary developmental work:** Autonomy vs. shame/doubt (Erikson) · language acquisition · emotional co-regulation · sensorimotor mastery

**Note on the current 2-year-old's profile:** Already finding Wally independently and sustaining attention in Richard Scarry's dense illustration pages. At age 2, this indicates unusually strong attentional control and pattern recognition — predicts well for obsessive focus, first-principles reasoning, and systems-heavy content. Feed it aggressively.

## Activity Map

| [[02 The Ten Targets\|Target]] | Core Approach | Products & Resources |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Efficacy** | Resist rescuing immediately. Let them struggle with lids, puzzles, stairs, construction. "Try again" before helping. Attribute success to effort: "you figured that out." | [Grimm's Rainbow](#resources) · [HABA Stacking Toys](#resources) · [Melissa & Doug Wooden Knob Puzzles](#resources) → [Orchard Toys Sequence Puzzles](#resources) · [Learning Tower (Little Partners)](#resources) |
| **Self-Compassion** | Model it audibly: "I dropped that. That's frustrating. I'll clean it up and try again." Engage with failures: "that was hard — what happened? what do we try?" | [When Sophie Gets Angry — Molly Bang](#resources) · [The Most Magnificent Thing — Ashley Spires](#resources) · [Jabari Jumps — Gaia Cornwall](#resources) |
| **Agency** | Constrained choices constantly: "pasta or sandwich?" not "what do you want?" Let natural consequences operate. | Montessori-aligned environment: low shelves, accessible toys, child-height hooks. The physical space communicates: "you can manage yourself." [Yoto Player](#resources) — they pick the card, they control the story. |
| **Grit** | Stay with tasks through frustration before offering help. Leave multi-session projects out — don't tidy them away. "We're still building this." | [Magna-Tiles 100-Piece Set](#resources) · [KAPLA Blocks 200-Piece](#resources) · [Brio Train Layout Sets](#resources) |
| **First-Principles Reasoning** | Answer "why" questions seriously every time. Narrate physical causation: "the water goes down because gravity pulls everything toward the ground." | [Thames & Kosmos My First Science Kit](#resources) · [ThinkFun Gravity Maze](#resources) (introduce components) · [Step2 Rain Showers Splash Pond](#resources) · [Melissa & Doug Magnetic Pattern Blocks and Board](#resources) — swap the card, figure out which shapes fit. |
| **Obsessive Focus** | Protect uninterrupted engagement. Don't redirect when absorbed. Don't break a good build session for a snack. | Behavioral, not product-based. Screen time max 1hr/day: [Bluey (ABC Kids Australia)](#resources) · [My Neighbor Totoro — Miyazaki 1988](#resources) |
| **Ambiguity Tolerance** | Read books that don't resolve neatly. Ask open questions; don't rush to answer them. | [Where the Wild Things Are — Maurice Sendak](#resources) · [Harold and the Purple Crayon — Crockett Johnson](#resources) · [Anno's Counting Book — Mitsumasa Anno](#resources) |
| **Genuine Curiosity** | Follow their curiosity, not your agenda. If obsessed with trucks, go maximally deep on trucks. The curiosity is the education. | [Richard Scarry — Extended Library](#resources) · [Where's Wally — Martin Handford full series](#resources) · [Anno's Animals — Mitsumasa Anno](#resources) · [Anno's Journey — Mitsumasa Anno](#resources) · [I Spy Series — Marzollo / Wick](#resources) |
| **Verbal Intelligence** | Narrate everything. Ask questions requiring more than yes/no. Dinner table: "best thing today? what was hard?" | [Rory's Story Cubes](#resources) · [Mo Willems — Pigeon & Elephant and Piggie](#resources) · [Dr. Seuss — Cat in the Hat, Green Eggs and Ham, Fox in Socks](#resources) |
| **Physical Hardship Tolerance** | Accept minor bumps without catastrophizing. Cold weather outdoor play in all weather. | [Pikler Triangle](#resources) · [Strider 12 Sport Balance Bike](#resources) · [Reima / Polarn O. Pyret Snowsuits](#resources) |

## [[03 Mental Models Progression\|Mental Models]] at This Stage

See [[03 Mental Models Progression#Ages 1–3 Foundational Cognition|Ages 1–3 section]].

Key installations: cause and effect (every physical session) · iteration (narrate retries) · systems/interdependence seed via [Richard Scarry — Extended Library](#resources).

**Most important mental model activity:** Ask "what do you think will happen?" before pouring water, stacking blocks, dropping anything. Then: "why do you think that?"
