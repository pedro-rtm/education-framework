# Age 1–3: Agency, Attention, and the Language Explosion

← [[05 Age 0-1]] | [[00 Education Framework|Index]] | Next: [[07 Age 4-7]]

**Current relevance:** 2-year-old is here now.

---

## Developmental Reality

Language is exploding. The first assertion of autonomous will ("no") is developmental, not misbehavior. Symbolic thinking is emerging — objects can represent other objects.

**Primary developmental work:** Autonomy vs. shame/doubt (Erikson) · language acquisition · emotional co-regulation · sensorimotor mastery

**Note on the current 2-year-old's profile:** Already finding Wally independently and sustaining attention in Richard Scarry's dense illustration pages. At age 2, this indicates unusually strong attentional control and pattern recognition — predicts well for obsessive focus, first-principles reasoning, and systems-heavy content. Feed it aggressively.

## [[03 Mental Models Progression\|Mental Models]] at This Stage

See [[03 Mental Models Progression#Ages 1–3 Foundational Cognition|Ages 1–3 section]].

Key installations: cause and effect (every physical session) · iteration (narrate retries) · systems/interdependence seed via [[#Resources\|Richard Scarry — Extended Library]].

**Most important mental model activity:** Ask "what do you think will happen?" before pouring water, stacking blocks, dropping anything. Then: "why do you think that?"
