# Age 0–1: Neural Foundations

← [[00 Education Framework|Back to Index]] | Next: [[06 Age 1-3]]

**Current relevance:** 6-month-old is here now.

---

## Developmental Reality

You are not instilling virtues at this age. The prefrontal cortex is essentially offline. What you are doing is wiring the neural substrate on which everything else will be built. Secure attachment predicts more downstream outcomes than any activity or product.

**The three real levers:**
1. Responsive caregiving → secure attachment → emotional regulation → all virtue development
2. Language saturation → vocabulary → reading → academic outcomes
3. Sensory richness → neural connectivity → perceptual and cognitive capacity

## Activity Map

| [[02 The Ten Targets\|Target]] | Core Approach | Products & Resources |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Efficacy seed** | Respond consistently to signals. Reliability teaches "my actions produce results." Respond promptly to distress — the research is unambiguous, this does not spoil infants. | [Lovevery Play Gym](#resources) for graduated reach/grasp challenges. |
| **Self-Compassion seed** | Soothe distress with warmth and physical contact. Teaches: emotional states are survivable. | [Ergobaby 360 Carrier](#resources) — hands-free skin-to-skin. |
| **Agency seed** | Let them reach, bat, grab, explore without constant redirection. Every successful grasp teaches "my intention produces results." | [Lovevery Play Gym](#resources). Replace hanging objects regularly — novelty maintains engagement. |
| **Genuine Curiosity seed** | High-contrast visual input; varied sensory stimulation. The brain is building the capacity to be interested. | **Montessori mobile sequence:** [Munari Mobile](#resources) (birth–6 wks) → [Octahedron Mobile](#resources) (6–8 wks) → [Gobbi Mobile](#resources) (8–12 wks) → [Dancer Mobile](#resources) (12–16 wks). [Wimmer-Ferguson High-Contrast Cards](#resources). [Lovevery Play Kit subscription](#resources). |
| **Verbal Intelligence seed** | Narrate constantly. Everything you do, name it. Questions, not just statements; varied vocabulary, not simplified. | Read aloud daily from birth: [Sandra Boynton books](#resources), [Karen Katz books](#resources), [Eric Carle — The Very Hungry Caterpillar](#resources). |
| **Obsessive Focus seed** | Do not interrupt sustained attention. When the baby is absorbed in studying an object, don't redirect. | [Montessori Treasure Basket](#resources) — natural objects of varied texture/weight/sound. Produces intense engaged exploration at 6 months. |
| **Physical Hardship seed** | Tummy time from the beginning. Don't catastrophize minor frustration. Skip bouncy seats that hold position — floor time builds effort. | [Boppy Pillow](#resources) for supported tummy time. Firm floor surface for free movement. |
| **All other targets** | Not yet applicable. Secure attachment is the entire curriculum. | [The Whole-Brain Child — Siegel & Bryson](#resources) · [Elevating Child Care — Janet Lansbury](#resources) · [The Science of Parenting — Margot Sunderland](#resources) · [Montessori from the Start — Lillard & Jessen](#resources) |

## [[03 Mental Models Progression\|Mental Models]] at This Stage

Pre-linguistic foundations only: cause and effect (every responsive interaction), object permanence (~8 months), categorization (varied objects/textures/sounds).

---

## [[04 Content Curriculum\|Content]] at This Stage

**Music:** Classical as ambient environment — Bach, Mozart, Debussy. Varied folk and world music.

**Screen time:** None before 18 months except video calls (AAP). Passive screens produce less neural development per hour than interaction with a responsive caregiver or physical objects.
