# Children's Education Framework

A living document for raising children with depth, agency, and the cognitive tools to navigate a complex world. Built for Pedro's two children: ages 2 and 6 months as of March 2026.

---

## Core Argument

> One film understood deeply is worth more than fifty watched passively. The goal is not exposure — it is the ability to *read* anything.

The framework rests on three interlocking layers that compound over time:

| Layer | What it is | Where to read |
|---|---|---|
| **Virtues & Skills** | Ten targets: who your child becomes | [[02 The Ten Targets]] |
| **Mental Models** | Cognitive tools: how they reason about anything | [[03 Mental Models Progression]] |
| **Content Curriculum** | What they engage with, and how deeply | [[04 Content Curriculum]] |

How these connect: virtues are the *disposition*, mental models are the *tools*, and content is the *medium* through which both are built. A child who watches Avatar: The Last Airbender once has watched a cartoon. A child who studies it — who understands Konietzko and DiMartino's decisions, the martial arts research behind each bending style, the deliberate narrative architecture of redemption across three seasons — has practiced first-principles reasoning, systems thinking, moral philosophy, and craft analysis simultaneously.

## Note on the 2-Year-Old's Profile

The 2-year-old already demonstrates sustained visual search in information-dense images (Where's Wally), absorbed attention in Richard Scarry's systems-heavy illustrations, and finds Wally independently. At age 2, this is a meaningful signal: strong attentional control, pattern recognition, and comfort with density. The framework accounts for this profile — see [[06 Age 1-3]] for specific recommendations.
