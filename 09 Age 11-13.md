## Developmental Reality

Identity formation begins in earnest. Peer pressure peaks. A child entering this phase with a real domain of competence, a self-analytical failure debrief practice, and genuine curiosity about ideas has a fundamentally different experience of adolescence than one without these. The self-compassion and self-efficacy work done earlier pays dividends here or the deficit is painfully visible.

## [[03 Mental Models Progression\|Mental Models]] at This Stage

See [[03 Mental Models Progression#Ages 11–13 Probabilistic and Epistemic Models|Ages 11–13 section]].

Key: **Probabilistic thinking** via games and sports statistics · **Survivorship bias** — history class as a survivorship bias exercise · **Confirmation bias** — "what would you need to see to change your mind?" · **Compounding** — make the math of daily practice visible.

**The Abraham Wald conversation:** Tell the WWII airplane armor story — the statistician who noticed planes returned with bullet holes in wings/fuselage not engines, and argued armor should go on the engines. Tell it at dinner. Ask them to find the error before revealing the answer.
