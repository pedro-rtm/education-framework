## Developmental Reality

Identity formation begins in earnest. Peer pressure peaks. A child entering this phase with a real domain of competence, a self-analytical failure debrief practice, and genuine curiosity about ideas has a fundamentally different experience of adolescence than one without these. The self-compassion and self-efficacy work done earlier pays dividends here or the deficit is painfully visible.

## Activity Map

| [[02 The Ten Targets\|Target]] | Core Approach | Products & Resources |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Efficacy** | Let them encounter genuinely hard problems without intervention. Real failure is the input — not difficulty followed by rescue. | [AMC 8 and AMC 10 Math Competitions](#resources) · First deployed coding project (Replit or GitHub) · Science fair entry · [Art of Problem Solving Intro to Algebra](#resources) · [Art of Problem Solving Intro to Counting and Probability](#resources) |
| **Self-Compassion** | Critical period — social cruelty peaks here. Tools need to be explicitly named. Introduce Stoic framing: what is and is not in your control. | [Meditations — Marcus Aurelius Gregory Hays Translation](#resources) — "this is what the most powerful man in the world wrote to himself when things were hard." One section together, discussed. Daily journaling: 10 min on what you told yourself about what happened. |
| **Agency** | Full accountability for academic performance and personal commitments. No rescuing. Post-mortem analytically: "what happened? what would you do differently? what's the plan?" | Responsibility for their own schedule, homework, social commitments. You are available to discuss, not to manage. |
| **Grit** | Something genuinely hard over 1–2 years where failure is possible and public. | [Go Board with Yunzi Stones](#resources) + [OGS Online Go Server](#resources) — takes years to understand. [Warhammer 40K Starter Set](#resources) — a single army takes months to paint. Competitive sport season, year-long. |
| **First-Principles Reasoning** | Formal introduction to logical structure. Derive before memorize. Distinguish proof from explanation. | [Art of Problem Solving Intro to Algebra](#resources) · Work through first 10 propositions of Euclid's Elements (ask what each proof assumes) · Build a trebuchet: calculate physics, build it, compare theory to result |
| **Obsessive Focus** | Connect the obsession to productive output. It should generate something now, not just consume. | Write an analysis of the game's mechanics. Write in the story universe. Coach younger kids in the sport. Deep work setup: dedicated workspace, no phone, 90-min blocks. |
| **Ambiguity Tolerance** | Philosophy of science. History as competing interpretations. The smartest people in history were consistently wrong about important things. | [Sophie's World — Jostein Gaarder](#resources) · [Godel Escher Bach — Douglas Hofstadter](#resources) (start now, finish over years) · The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (simplified version first) |
| **Genuine Curiosity** | Cross-domain connection. Where does their obsession touch other fields? The most interesting questions live at intersections. | [Brilliant.org Advanced Tracks](#resources) · [3Blue1Brown YouTube](#resources) · [Feynman Messenger Lecture Series YouTube](#resources) |
| **Verbal Intelligence** | Real-time argument construction under pressure. Improv and debate as explicit practices. | [Competitive Debate WSDC or British Parliamentary](#resources) — argue positions you disagree with. [Improv Theater Classes](#resources) — real program, real audience. [Model UN Annual Participation](#resources) |
| **Physical Hardship Tolerance** | Systematic challenge with documented progression. Multi-day wilderness. Competitive contact sport. Cold protocol. | 4–5 day backpacking with [Osprey Kestrel 38 Backpack](#resources) — map and compass navigation, no GPS. [Cold Plunge Protocol](#resources). [Martial Arts Tournament Entry](#resources) — preparation is the point. |

## [[03 Mental Models Progression\|Mental Models]] at This Stage

See [[03 Mental Models Progression#Ages 11–13 Probabilistic and Epistemic Models|Ages 11–13 section]].

Key: **Probabilistic thinking** via games and sports statistics · **Survivorship bias** — history class as a survivorship bias exercise · **Confirmation bias** — "what would you need to see to change your mind?" · **Compounding** — make the math of daily practice visible.

**The Abraham Wald conversation:** Tell the WWII airplane armor story — the statistician who noticed planes returned with bullet holes in wings/fuselage not engines, and argued armor should go on the engines. Tell it at dinner. Ask them to find the error before revealing the answer.
