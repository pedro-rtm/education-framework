summary: "Agency as birthright. Lindgren's children act — they invent, defy, run, mourn, and choose — in a register rare in contemporary children's literature."
whyItBelongs: "Lindgren's protagonists are not incidentally brave; they are structurally autonomous. Pippi Longstocking is anarchic self-efficacy incarnate — a nine-year-old alone with a horse, a monkey, and a suitcase of gold, declining the adult world's framing of childhood. Emil, Ronja, and the Lionheart brothers extend the pattern: children who act, with consequences. The Brothers Lionheart confronts death as literally as any children's book ever has. Lindgren trusts the child to metabolise mortality, injustice, and moral ambiguity. In a curriculum where most material soft-pedals the child, Lindgren is calibration."
