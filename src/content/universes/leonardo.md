phases:
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: recommended
    title: "Leonardo the Observer"
    description: "Introduction through visual wonder: his drawings of flying machines, anatomical sketches, water studies. DK or National Geographic children's books on Leonardo. Focus: he drew EVERYTHING he saw — birds in flight, water in motion, muscles under skin. Observation as the foundation of both art and science. 'The noblest pleasure is the joy of understanding.'"
    mode: absorb
    goals:
      - "See that drawing is a tool for thinking, not just decoration."
      - "Understand that art and science were the same activity for Leonardo."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: core
    title: "Leonardo the Engineer-Artist"
    description: "Walter Isaacson's biography (selected chapters) or a comprehensive illustrated guide. His notebooks: mirror writing, anatomical dissections, war machines, hydraulic engineering, painting technique — all in the same pages. The concept of sfumato (blurred boundaries) applies to knowledge itself: the border between art and engineering is a human invention, not a natural law. Connect to Material Culture: Leonardo understood materials, forces, and construction at a level that modern specialists rarely achieve."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Understand polymath thinking: how knowledge in one domain feeds breakthroughs in another."
      - "Start a visual notebook practice: sketch ideas, not just write them."
    projects:
      - "Keep a Leonardo-style notebook for one month: observations, sketches, questions, ideas — all mixed together."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: recommended
    title: "Leonardo the Designer"
    description: "Study Leonardo as industrial designer before industrial design existed. His approach to any problem: observe nature → understand the mechanism → design a solution → prototype → iterate. This IS the design-fabrication pipeline in the Material Culture curriculum. Read selections from the Codex Leicester or Codex Atlanticus (translated). Critique: where did Leonardo fail? Many designs never worked. The gap between vision and execution is itself a lesson."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
      - "Apply Leonardo's observe-understand-design-prototype cycle to a real project."
      - "Understand that creative failure (designs that don't work) is productive, not wasteful."
