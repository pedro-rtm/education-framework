summary: "Winnie-the-Pooh. The quiet-mind canon — friendship, temperament, and the wisdom of doing very little, very well."
whyItBelongs: "Milne's Hundred Acre Wood is a typology of temperament disguised as a children's book. Pooh is calm presence; Piglet is anxiety; Eeyore is depression; Tigger is mania; Rabbit is control; Owl is pseudo-expertise; Kanga is maternal steadiness. Reading Pooh over years lets a child recognise these dispositions in themselves and others — it is the earliest relational-intelligence text in the canon. The prose is also a masterclass in restraint: nothing is wasted, nothing is sentimental, the jokes are usually at the expense of complexity (The House at Pooh Corner ends with one of the most delicate farewells in English literature). Via-negativa register — happiness is arrived at by removing striving, not by adding anything. Strip the Disney overlay entirely."
