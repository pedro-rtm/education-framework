phases:
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: recommended
    title: "Geological time and deep time"
    description: "Geological time as concept. Fossils, rock layers, deep time. Attenborough's nature documentaries as ecosystem introduction. 'The Earth is 4.5 billion years old — if that were a day, humans arrive at 11:59:58 PM.'"
    mode: absorb
    goals:
      - "Grasp deep time: the Earth is ancient, humans are recent, and the timescales dwarf human history."
      - "Develop intuition for ecosystems as interconnected systems, not collections of animals."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: core
    title: "Cycles, feedback loops, and planetary boundaries"
    description: "Carbon cycle. Water cycle. Feedback loops in climate (ice albedo, permafrost methane). Planetary boundaries framework (Rockström). What's a tipping point? Where are we relative to them?"
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Understand carbon and water cycles as physical systems with feedback loops."
      - "Know the nine planetary boundaries and which ones humanity has already crossed."
      - "Distinguish a tipping point from a gradual change."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: core
    title: "Climate science from primary sources"
    description: "IPCC summaries (not the full reports — the Summaries for Policymakers). Wallace-Wells's The Uninhabitable Earth. Evaluate: where is Wallace-Wells being precise and where is he being rhetorical? Climate models as Maps ≠ Territory. Resource limits vs. technological optimism."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Read IPCC summaries as primary sources — understand what they claim and what they don't."
      - "Distinguish rhetorical climate writing from scientific climate writing."
      - "Evaluate climate models as models: useful approximations with known failure modes."
  - phaseId: age-17-18
    tier: core
    title: "Earth systems as complex adaptive systems"
    description: "Earth systems as complex adaptive systems. Gaia hypothesis (Lovelock) — useful model or mysticism? Critique climate models: where do they fail? What do they assume? Energy transition as earth systems problem, not just engineering problem."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Understand the Earth as a complex adaptive system, not a simple machine."
      - "Evaluate Lovelock's Gaia hypothesis: where is it scientifically productive and where does it become metaphor?"
      - "Connect energy transition to earth systems — climate is not just an engineering problem."
