---
label: "Cooking"
kind: practice-domain
summary: "Food as system — chemistry, nutrition, culture, hospitality, and the independence that comes from feeding yourself and others well."
whyItBelongs: "Cooking builds agency, self-reliance, and social capability simultaneously. A person who can cook dinner for eight has a social superpower that compounds through every decade of life. It is also the most accessible daily practice of the engagement cycle: absorb a recipe, interrogate the technique, make the dish, and iterate."
tags: [activity]
languages: [english, spanish]
targets: [agency, physical-system-mastery, creative-production, relational-intelligence]
models: [cause-effect, leverage, feedback-loops]
intensity: low
socialValue: bridge
phases:
  - phaseId: age-4-7
    tier: core
    title: "Kitchen helper — measuring, mixing, tasting"
    description: "Helping in the kitchen: measuring ingredients, mixing batter, tasting and seasoning. Understanding that food comes from ingredients combined with technique, not from packages."
    mode: absorb
    goals:
      - "Normalise cooking as something everyone does, not a gendered or specialised activity."
      - "Build comfort with kitchen tools, heat, and the idea that food is made, not bought."
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: core
    title: "5 meals without a recipe"
    description: "By 10, they can cook five complete meals from memory without consulting a recipe. Scrambled eggs, pasta with sauce, rice and stir-fry, a simple soup, and a salad with dressing. These are independence skills."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Achieve genuine cooking independence — they can feed themselves a real meal."
      - "Understand food as chemistry: why does bread rise? Why does meat brown?"
    projects:
      - "Cook one family dinner per month, start to finish, without help."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: core
    title: "Meal planning, budget cooking, and cultural range"
    description: "Plan a week of meals within a budget. Cook from multiple cultural traditions. Understand nutrition as a system, not a set of rules."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
      - "Experience budget constraint as a creative challenge, not a limitation."
      - "Expand cultural range: Japanese, Indian, Mexican, Middle Eastern — cooking IS cultural literacy."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: core
    title: "Dinner party for 8"
    description: "The milestone: plan, shop, cook, and host a dinner party for 8 people. Timing, presentation, and hosting are the skills — not just the cooking."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Exercise hospitality as a social skill — managing timing, conversation, and multiple dishes simultaneously."
      - "Understand that cooking for others is an act of service that builds relationships."
  - phaseId: age-17-18
    tier: core
    title: "Autonomous food system"
    description: "They manage their own nutrition: meal planning, shopping, cooking, and eating well without parental infrastructure. This is the 'furnishes first space on a budget' competency applied to food."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "An autonomous food system that survives the transition to university — they eat well because they can cook, not because a dining hall exists."
---
