whyItBelongs: "The framework has no modernist fiction. Joyce fills that gap across a spectrum of accessibility. Dubliners (1914) is a collection of short stories about Dublin that are among the most precisely written in English — accessible at 14, devastating at any age. Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man traces Stephen Dedalus from childhood to artistic independence through prose that evolves with the protagonist's consciousness. Ulysses is the Mount Everest of the novel — one day in Dublin, 18 episodes, each in a different style, containing everything: comedy, tragedy, philosophy, pornography, parody, stream of consciousness, and the most famous closing word in literature. The framework should produce a reader capable of attempting Ulysses. Whether they summit is their business."

The snow falling 'upon all the living and the dead' is the most precise image of mortality in literature. Read the full collection — the stories build cumulatively. Discuss: what is the 'paralysis' Joyce diagnoses in Dublin? Where do you see it in your own city, your own life?"
