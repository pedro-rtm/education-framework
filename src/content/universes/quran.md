summary: "The foundational text of 1.8 billion people, read as poetic and rhetorical artefact. Rhyming prose (saj'), Meccan intensity, Medinan legal argumentation, and the long intertextual conversation with the Hebrew Bible."
whyItBelongs: "The Quran is the most widely read book on earth that most Western-educated readers have never opened. Read as literature, it is structurally unlike the Bible — not a narrative collection but a layered address, where God speaks in the first person and stories are referenced rather than told sequentially. Its Arabic is famously untranslatable (the rhyme-prose structure collapses on translation); M.A.S. Abdel Haleem's Oxford edition is the closest English equivalent, with scholarly apparatus that respects literary form. Cultural literacy alone argues for engagement: the Quran's imagery, cadences, and Joseph/Moses/Jesus narratives are the substrate of a civilisation whose arts, politics, and philosophy we interact with daily. Read alongside the Hebrew Bible universe to see the intertextuality directly."
