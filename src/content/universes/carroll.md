kind: creator-canon
summary: "The Oxford logician who weaponised nonsense. Alice is the first text that teaches a child that the rules of the world are themselves contingent."
whyItBelongs: "Carroll was a mathematician writing what looks like children's whimsy but is actually a stress test of logic, identity, and language. Alice's body changes size (what is 'I' if the referent keeps changing?). The Queen shouts 'Sentence first — verdict afterwards!' (causal inversion as satire of institutions). The Cheshire Cat is a grin without a cat (map without territory). Jabberwocky proves that syntax carries meaning independent of vocabulary. Hofstadter's Gödel, Escher, Bach is openly Carrollian — its Achilles-and-Tortoise dialogues are direct descendants. Read Carroll at age 4-7 and it is delightful nonsense; re-read at 11-13 and it is the entry text to formal logic, paradox, and self-reference."

Memorise Jabberwocky: the poem is nonsense vocabulary inside perfect syntactic scaffolding, which makes it the best argument that grammar carries meaning without content."

Gardner was a logician and Carroll devotee; his annotations expose the mathematical substrate — the tea party is a riddle about infinite regress, the caucus race is an electoral satire, the entire book is stuffed with puzzles Carroll set for his Oxford colleagues. Bridge to hofstadter."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
