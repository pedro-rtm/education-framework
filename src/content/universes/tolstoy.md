whyItBelongs: "The framework has Dostoevsky for Russian moral psychology — extreme situations, guilt, redemption. Tolstoy fills the complementary gap: history as system. War and Peace asks whether Napoleon shaped history or was carried by it — whether individuals are causes or symptoms of historical forces. Anna Karenina applies the same lens to private life: are we authors of our choices or products of our social systems? The Historical Cycles universe already covers Toynbee and Spengler; Tolstoy dramatises their question in fiction. Distinct from Dostoevsky in every way: where Dostoevsky is fevered, Tolstoy is panoramic; where Dostoevsky explores extremes, Tolstoy explores the ordinary."
