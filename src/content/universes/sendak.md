summary: "Emotional regulation as curriculum. Sendak is the picture-book artist who took children's inner lives — rage, hunger, fear — seriously, and made them legible."
whyItBelongs: "Where the Wild Things Are is one of the most psychologically precise children's books ever drawn. Max's rage, the monsters as projection, the rumpus, the return to a dinner still warm — the book is a complete cycle of affect regulation in thirty-seven pages. In the Night Kitchen is about hunger, dream logic, and the body. Outside Over There — Sendak's own favourite — is the strangest of the three: a sister whose baby brother is stolen by goblins, an ice-cold Mozart-scored rescue. Together the trilogy gives the child a vocabulary for feelings that precede language. Sendak is the rare picture-book artist whose books reward re-reading across decades."
