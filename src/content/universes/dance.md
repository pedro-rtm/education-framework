summary: "Body as instrument of expression, social connection through movement, and rhythmic intelligence across cultural forms."
whyItBelongs: "Dance develops physical confidence, social competence, cultural literacy, and improvisational intelligence simultaneously. A person who dances well navigates social situations that others find paralysing. It is one of the most directly applicable social skills in the framework."
tags: [activity, physical]
languages: [english, spanish]
targets: [physical-system-mastery, communication-mastery, adaptability, relational-intelligence]
models: [complementary-opposites, feedback-loops]
intensity: medium
socialValue: bridge
phases:
  - phaseId: age-4-7
    tier: core
    title: "Creative movement, freestyle, and ballet foundations"
    description: "Creative movement class (responding to tempos, mirroring partners). Freestyle dance at home as family culture. Ballet foundations at 6-7 for movement grammar. Flamenco palmas for rhythmic awareness."
    mode: absorb
    goals:
      - "Establish the habit of moving to music before self-consciousness arrives."
      - "Build body awareness and rhythm without technical rigidity."
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: core
    title: "First structured style and body isolations"
    description: "Choose a style based on pull: flamenco, hip-hop, Latin basics, swing, or Afrobeats. Body isolations begin — what separates 'doing steps' from 'dancing.' Ballet continues if producing joy."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Develop genuine style in at least one form."
      - "Build body isolations — chest, hips, shoulders moving independently."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: foundational
    title: "Social dance becomes urgent"
    description: "Before their first school dance: salsa, bachata, merengue basics. Lead/follow fundamentals — both roles. Attend a social dance event. This is social insurance for the next 70 years."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Never be the person standing against the wall."
      - "Learn that leading is communication through gentle pressure, following is interpretation — not passivity."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: core
    title: "Competence across contexts"
    description: "Can dance at a Latin party (salsa), a club (freestyle), a formal event (basic waltz), and a family gathering (whatever is playing). Improvisation emphasis — social dance is 90% improvisation."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Move well to unfamiliar music in any social context."
      - "Dance competence is achieved when it is invisible — they simply dance, naturally."
  - phaseId: age-17-18
    tier: core
    title: "Dance as social identity"
    description: "Dance is one of the most valuable social skills at university. The person who dances confidently makes friends faster and navigates social settings more naturally."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Social independence made physical: they dance because they enjoy it, not because they are performing."
