summary: "Borges's closest collaborator — the Argentine architect of the fantastic novella and the inventor of virtual reality fiction."
whyItBelongs: "Bioy Casares is the quietest giant of the River Plate tradition. Borges's closest friend and collaborator for five decades (together they invented Bustos Domecq), Bioy wrote with a different temperature — Borges is cold and labyrinthine, Bioy is warmer and more narratively traditional, but just as structurally radical. La invención de Morel (1940) is the key text: 100 pages, a fugitive on a deserted island discovers that the people he sees are recordings — Morel has invented a machine that records full sensory experience, and the price of eternal life is that the recorded subjects die. Borges's prologue calls it a perfect novel. It prefigured Westworld, Black Mirror, The Matrix, Inception and the entire virtual reality literary genre by seventy years. Plan de evasión (1945) is the prison-island novel; Historias fantásticas gathers the best of his short fiction. For a young reader coming from Borges, Bioy is the necessary complement — the same conceptual apparatus embedded in more fully narrative forms. Spanish is the original; no translation needed."
