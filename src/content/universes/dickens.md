summary: "Institutional critique, poverty, justice, and the invention of social fiction — the original novelist who made readers see the systems that created suffering."
whyItBelongs: "Dickens fills the gap of the English social novel. Hugo gives you revolutionary France; Dickens gives you Industrial England. His contribution is making invisible systems visible: the workhouse (Oliver Twist), the legal system (Bleak House), the prison (Little Dorrit), the education system (Hard Times). Dickens didn't just describe poverty — he made the middle class see it, feel it, and demand reform. For a framework that teaches systems thinking, Dickens is the literary proof that seeing systems clearly is the first step to changing them."
