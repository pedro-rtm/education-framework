whyItBelongs: "Hemingway is the anti-Tolkien. Where Tolkien builds elaborately, Hemingway strips away. His iceberg theory — 'the dignity of movement of an iceberg is due to only one-eighth of it being above water' — is the most important writing principle the framework can teach. Every sentence earns its place; every omission is deliberate. For a framework that values communication mastery, Hemingway provides the limit case: maximum meaning from minimum words. His subjects — war, loss, masculinity, courage, the sea — are explored with a restraint that teaches the reader to find meaning in gaps."
