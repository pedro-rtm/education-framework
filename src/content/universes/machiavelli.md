whyItBelongs: "The framework teaches power structures as Model #16 but has no primary text for political realism. Machiavelli fills that gap. The Prince is not a manual for tyrants — it is a diagnostic tool: how does power actually work, stripped of how we wish it worked? The Discourses on Livy extend the analysis to republican governance. Together they provide the analytical framework for evaluating every political system the student will encounter. A child who reads Machiavelli at 14 understands that leaders' stated motives and actual motives diverge — and has the vocabulary to describe how."
