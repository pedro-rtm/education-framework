summary: "Language as the protagonist. Thurber's fables weaponise diction, internal rhyme, and cadence until prose itself becomes plot."
whyItBelongs: "Thurber is the rare children's author whose material object is the sound and shape of English. The 13 Clocks proceeds by invented words and rhythmic sentences that prefigure Nabokov's delight in surface. The Wonderful O is a linguistic thought experiment — remove one letter and half a culture collapses — which teaches, without lecturing, that vocabulary is infrastructure. His New Yorker pieces then transfer the same ear from fairy-tale to domestic comedy, showing the child that the craft continues into adult prose. Few other authors let a reader feel, this early, that words are material to be worked."
