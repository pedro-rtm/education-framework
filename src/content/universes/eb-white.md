summary: "Three perfect children's novels by a writer who also co-authored the definitive English style guide. Prose as craft, friendship as ethics, mortality handled honestly."
whyItBelongs: "E.B. White wrote Charlotte's Web, Stuart Little, The Trumpet of the Swan — and, with William Strunk, The Elements of Style. The same hand. This is unusual: most great children's writers are not also the authorities on prose economy. Reading White as a child, then returning to Strunk & White in adolescence, reveals that the clarity of the novels was not accident but craft. Charlotte's Web is also the canonical children's book about death — Charlotte dies, and the book does not flinch. 'She never moved again. Next day, as the Ferris wheel was being taken apart and the race horses were being loaded into vans and the entertainers were packing up their belongings and driving away in their trailers, Charlotte died.' A child who has absorbed that paragraph has received a first, gentle, accurate lesson about mortality and the dignity of how lives end."

    mode: absorb

Begin to notice that White never wastes a word."

Read Elements alongside a selection of White's essays — 'Once More to the Lake' is the canonical personal essay. The child who loved Charlotte as a seven-year-old now discovers that the prose they loved was the product of explicit craft principles. Meta-recognition of how writing works."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
