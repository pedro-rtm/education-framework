summary: "Metafiction and time theft. Ende's two central books — Momo and The Neverending Story — diagnose the modern attention economy decades before it had a name."
whyItBelongs: "Momo (1973) is structurally an allegory of industrial time capture: the Men in Grey persuade adults to bank their minutes, which are then drained and consumed elsewhere. Read in 2026, it reads as if written about smartphones. The Neverending Story is a metafictional recursion — Bastian enters the book he is reading, and the boundary between reader and story becomes the plot. Together the two books give the child two of the most important mental models available: (1) attention is the scarce resource modern systems extract, and (2) the frame of a story is not separate from the story. Jim Button is the lighter early Ende — a pure adventure with steam trains and dragons. Miguel Sáenz's Spanish translations are canonical."
