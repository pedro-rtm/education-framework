Jane Eyre is the origin of feminist self-assertion in English fiction — 'I am no bird; and no net ensnares me' — a governess with no money, no family, and no status who refuses to surrender her moral independence to love, money, or convention. Wuthering Heights is the anti-Jane Eyre: passion without restraint, love as destruction, nature as mirror of emotional extremes. Together they provide complementary models of female agency: Jane's disciplined self-respect and Catherine's ungovernable intensity. Neither is wrong. Both are instructive."
