whyItBelongs: "Aesop is the primordial behavioural-economics textbook. Each fable is a single-variable experiment: alter one incentive, watch the consequence. The Tortoise and the Hare encodes compounding; The Boy Who Cried Wolf encodes reputation decay and Bayesian updating; The Fox and the Grapes is textbook cognitive dissonance; The Ant and the Grasshopper is time preference. Read young, the morals feel intuitive; re-read at age 11-13 alongside Kahneman and the same fables become diagnostic tools for bias."

    mode: dim
    goals:

    title: "Aesop + Socratic discussion"
    description: "Revisit the canon asking harder questions. Was the ant cruel? Was the grasshopper's mistake really laziness, or miscalibrated time-preference? Use fables as moral-reasoning drills — identify the implicit ethical claim and test it against edge cases."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:

    title: "Aesop through the behavioural-science lens"
    description: "Re-read alongside Kahneman (Thinking Fast and Slow) and the behavioural-science universe. The fables become taxonomic specimens."
    mode: critique
    goals:

      - "Critique Aesop: which morals reflect enduring human nature, which are culturally contingent?"
