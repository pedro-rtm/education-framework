---
label: Visual Design Fundamentals
kind: maker-system
summary: "Drawing, composition, colour theory, typography, and spatial design — the grammar of visual thinking."
whyItBelongs: "The framework develops communication mastery across written, oral, digital, and performative media but has no visual art system. Visual thinking is a distinct cognitive mode: it trains spatial reasoning, pattern recognition, aesthetic judgment, and the ability to communicate complex ideas through image. A person who can sketch an idea, compose a photograph, evaluate a design, and understand why one object looks right and another doesn't has a cognitive tool that words alone cannot provide. Visual literacy is the missing medium in Communication Mastery."
tags: [maker, english, stem]
targets: [creative-production, communication-mastery, deep-curiosity, self-awareness, first-principles-reasoning]
models: [feedback-loops, complementary-opposites, via-negativa, emergence]
intensity: medium
socialValue: broad
phases:
  - phaseId: age-4-7
    tier: recommended
    title: "Seeing and Drawing"
    description: "Drawing as observation, not talent. 'Draw what you see, not what you think you see.' Provide quality materials: good paper, real pencils (not crayons only), watercolours. Nature drawing: sketch a leaf, a shell, a pet. The act of drawing forces looking — 5 minutes of drawing teaches more about a flower's structure than 30 minutes of reading about it. Photography as seeing: give them a camera (phone is fine). Weekly photo challenge: 'capture something that shows [concept].'"
    mode: make
    goals:
      - "Establish drawing as a thinking tool, not a talent-dependent art form."
      - "Begin seeing composition: what's in the frame, what's left out."
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: core
    title: "Fundamentals of Composition"
    description: "The elements of visual design, made practical: line, shape, value (light and dark), colour, texture, space. Rule of thirds in photography. Contrast creates interest. Symmetry vs. asymmetry. Sketch-noting: taking notes as words + drawings. Data visualisation basics: read and create simple charts. Mind mapping for projects. If they enjoy drawing: observational drawing exercises (draw from life, not imagination). If they prefer digital: introduce Canva or similar for presentation design."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Understand and apply basic composition principles."
      - "Use sketch-noting as a learning tool alongside written notes."
    projects:
      - "Redesign something ugly: take a poorly designed poster, flyer, or slide and make it better. Articulate what changed and why."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: core
    title: "Colour Theory + Design Thinking"
    description: "Josef Albers' Interaction of Color (adapted): colours change depending on context. Warm vs. cool, complementary vs. analogous, value contrast vs. hue contrast. Apply to: presentation design, room layout, clothing coordination, website design. Design thinking process: empathise, define, ideate, prototype, test. Infographic creation: take data and make it visual. Typography basics: why fonts matter, what makes text readable, hierarchy through size and weight."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Understand colour interaction and apply it to real design decisions."
      - "Create infographics that communicate data visually."
    projects:
      - "Design an infographic on a topic they care about. Data must be real. Design must be clear."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: recommended
    title: "Design Systems and Visual Critique"
    description: "Study design systems: Bauhaus (form follows function), Dieter Rams' 10 principles of good design, Apple's design language. Tear down well-designed and badly-designed objects (connects to Material Culture industrial design content). Architecture as lived space: proportion, light, circulation, how a building makes you feel. Photography deepened: composition, lighting, post-processing. Portfolio design: their personal website and project presentations should look intentional, not default."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
      - "Evaluate design quality with vocabulary and criteria, not just 'I like it.'"
      - "Apply design principles to their own portfolio and presentations."
  - phaseId: age-17-18
    tier: recommended
    title: "Visual Communication as Professional Competence"
    description: "By 18, visual communication is a professional skill: clear diagrams, compelling presentations, readable documents, intentional portfolio design. They understand that visual clarity reflects clarity of thought. Can critique any visualisation. Has a personal aesthetic they can articulate."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Produce professional-quality visual communication across media."
      - "Articulate design decisions: why this layout, this colour, this typeface."
