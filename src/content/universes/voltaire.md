whyItBelongs: "Candide is 120 pages long and contains more ideas per page than most novels contain in total. Voltaire wrote it in three days (allegedly) as an attack on Leibniz's optimism — the idea that 'this is the best of all possible worlds.' Candide travels the globe, encounters every form of human cruelty and natural disaster, and concludes: 'We must cultivate our garden.' This is the framework's most concise statement of agency — not optimism, not pessimism, but the decision to do useful work within your sphere of influence regardless of the state of the world. For a framework built on agency as a core target, Voltaire provides its philosophical origin story in a text short enough to read in an afternoon."
