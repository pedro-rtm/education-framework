summary: "Grimm, Andersen, Perrault in their original unsanitised form. The canonical European folk corpus — darker, stranger, and more morally complex than the Disney layer."
whyItBelongs: "Fairy tales are the pre-literate Europe's distilled psychology — stepmothers, forests, wolves, mirrors, doors-that-should-not-be-opened. The original Grimm texts are violent and amoral; Andersen is melancholic and often cruel (The Little Match Girl does not end well); Perrault is courtly and satirical. The sanitisation is the mistake: the darkness is the curriculum. Bettelheim's Uses of Enchantment argues the grim material is psychologically necessary — children metabolise fear through story. Reading the originals, then comparing to Disney, trains inversion (what was changed, and why?) and complementary-opposites (good/evil, beauty/ugliness, wish/consequence)."

    title: "Grimm's Complete Fairy Tales (Jack Zipes translation) + Andersen (Tiina Nunnally)"
    description: "The full canon, unabridged, scholarly translations. Zipes is the definitive Grimm (preserves the coarseness and moral ambiguity of the 1857 edition). Nunnally's Andersen restores the Danish melancholy. Read beyond the hits — the obscure tales (The Juniper Tree, The Goose-Girl, The Shadow) are the strangest and most formative."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Map recurring motifs across tales (youngest child succeeds, kindness rewarded, greed punished, transformations)."
      - "Distinguish Grimm's structural morality from Andersen's Christian-melancholic register."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: core
    title: "Originals vs. Disney — a comparative study"
    description: "Structured comparison. Read the Grimm Cinderella (stepsisters mutilate their feet, are blinded by birds) next to Disney's. Do the same for Snow White, Little Mermaid (Andersen's ends with her dissolving into sea foam), Sleeping Beauty (earlier versions include rape). What was sanitised? What was it protecting the child from — or the adult from? This is a masterclass in cultural editing and the ethics of adaptation."
    mode: critique
    cautions: "The unsanitised originals include sexual violence in some versions (early Sleeping Beauty, Bluebeard). Screen before recommending a specific edition."
    goals:
      - "Articulate what Disney added, removed, and why."
      - "Take a position: is sanitisation respect for the child, or condescension?"
  - phaseId: age-14-16

    title: "Bruno Bettelheim, The Uses of Enchantment + Angela Carter, The Bloody Chamber"
    description: "Two adult lenses. Bettelheim reads fairy tales as psychoanalytic primers (contested but influential). Carter rewrites them as feminist-gothic short stories — the same skeletons, new organs. This closes the arc from childhood absorption to critical literary engagement."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Evaluate Bettelheim's psychoanalytic readings against alternative (feminist, structuralist) frames."
      - "Analyse Carter's reworkings as a demonstration that old narratives remain generative."
