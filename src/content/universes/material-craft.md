---
label: Material Culture & Craft
kind: maker-system
summary: "Understanding how everyday objects are made, repairing before replacing, and developing the material competence that liberates from consumer dependence."
whyItBelongs: "Material literacy is the bridge between physical competence and philosophical independence. The person who understands how things are made, can repair what breaks, and evaluates objects by construction rather than brand is free in a way that the person who can only buy is not. This system develops agency, first-principles reasoning, and physical mastery simultaneously."
tags: [maker, stem]
targets: [agency, deep-curiosity, first-principles-reasoning, physical-system-mastery, resilient-self-efficacy]
models: [cause-effect, incentives, feedback-loops, inversion, via-negativa]
intensity: medium
socialValue: broad
substitutes: [lego, minecraft]
phases:
  - phaseId: age-4-7
    tier: core
    title: "Observe & Name"
    description: "Monthly visits to see how things are made (bakery, cobbler, ceramics). Weekly object conversations. First cooking and sewing. Gardening seed-to-plate. Energy questions: where does the light come from? Things are made by people — every manufactured object was designed and built by someone."
    mode: interrogate
    goals:
      - "Establish the reflex that objects have origins, materials, and makers."
      - "Build comfort with tools, heat, needles, and real materials."
    projects:
      - "Cook one simple meal per week by age 7."
      - "Sew a button and make a fabric pouch."
      - "Grow one food plant from seed to plate."
  - phaseId: age-8-10
    tier: core
    title: "Deconstruct & Begin to Repair"
    description: "Monthly object autopsies (cut open a sneaker). Basic repair skills (sewing, kintsugi, shoe goo). Electricity fundamentals (build a flashlight, trace power from plant to socket). House systems awareness (plumbing, heating). Vehicle curiosity (engine basics, mechanic visit). Price vs. value analysis."
    mode: make
    goals:
      - "Develop the analytical habit: what is this made of, how was it assembled, why did it fail?"
      - "Understand basic electrical circuits, energy systems, and house infrastructure."
      - "Begin distinguishing price from value."
    projects:
      - "Object autopsy: cut open a cheap and expensive sneaker, compare construction."
      - "Build a working flashlight from components."
      - "Kintsugi repair of a broken ceramic."
  - phaseId: age-11-13
    tier: foundational
    title: "Craft Skills, Technical Systems & Economics of Objects"
    description: "Actual craft: sewing garments, leatherwork, cobbling basics. Build a PC. Wire a plug, fix a tap, change a tire. Understand combustion engines and energy systems. Supply chain analysis. Fast fashion investigation. Wabi-sabi, William Morris, hedonic adaptation as named concepts."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Develop genuine craft skills in at least two material domains."
      - "Build and understand the computer you use daily."
      - "Understand residential electrical, plumbing, and mechanical systems."
      - "Analyse the economics of everyday objects — who made it, what were they paid, what is the true cost?"
    projects:
      - "Build a PC from selected components."
      - "Sew a bag from a pattern."
      - "Make a leather belt or wallet."
      - "Trace the full supply chain of one everyday object."
  - phaseId: age-14-16
    tier: foundational
    title: "Advanced Craft, Fabrication & Design"
    description: "Garment tailoring, shoe-making, bag construction. CNC and laser cutting. Industrial design fundamentals. Home automation. Solar charger build. Vehicle mechanics deepened (brakes, EV architecture). Household energy audit. Full design-fabrication pipeline. Thoreau, Japanese craft philosophy."
    mode: dim
    goals:
      - "Produce functional objects through the full design-fabrication pipeline."
      - "Understand advanced manufacturing methods (subtractive vs. additive)."
      - "Master household energy, electrical, and mechanical systems."
      - "Integrate philosophical frameworks: cost-as-life-exchanged, shokunin kishitsu, wabi-sabi."
    projects:
      - "Make a pair of sandals or espadrilles from raw materials."
      - "Design and fabricate a functional object: sketch to CAD to finished product."
      - "Build a solar charger."
      - "Conduct a household energy audit with recommendations."
  - phaseId: age-17-18
    tier: foundational
    title: "Self-Sufficiency as Identity"
    description: "Full material self-sufficiency: 20+ meals, garment repair, object evaluation, troubleshooting before calling professionals. Industrial design literacy. Launch project: furnish first independent space with budget and intention. Capability liberates."
    mode: critique
    goals:
      - "Material competence is integrated into identity, not performed as a skill."
      - "Can evaluate any manufactured object by construction, materials, and economics."
      - "Repair reflex is automatic: 'Can I fix this?' before 'Where can I buy a new one?'"
    projects:
      - "Furnish first independent living space: explicit budget, William Morris test, systems setup."
---
