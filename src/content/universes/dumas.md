Dumas fills the gap of pure narrative architecture: a protagonist who plans across decades, deploys resources strategically, and discovers that revenge corrodes the avenger. The Three Musketeers adds friendship, loyalty, and political intrigue at a lighter register. Both are accessible earlier than most literary fiction because the plotting is irresistible."
