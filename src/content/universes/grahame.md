whyItBelongs: "Grahame's one great book is a meditation on belonging and temperament. Rat loves the river; Mole loves home; Badger loves solitude; Toad loves whatever is loudest this week. Four characters, four stable dispositions — the book is an early primer on personality as complementary opposites. The pastoral register teaches a child that contentment is a legitimate subject for literature (a counterweight to dopamine-driven narrative). The Toad plot is comic, but the core of the book is the Piper at the Gates of Dawn chapter — numinous, slightly strange, unforgettable. Pastoral-friendship register pairs with milne and eb-white."
